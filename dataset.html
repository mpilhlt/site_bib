The SCC Resource Explorer is an interactive bibliographic database, which provides a collection of primary and secondary resources, dedicated to the Congregation of the Council, one of the main administrative bodies of the Church after the Council of Trent. The database contains more than thousand entities, related to historical and legal aspects of the Congregation of the Council and Roman Curia. The SCC Resource Explorer is an ongoing project and the database enriches with new entities in cooperation with the international academic community of historians, legal and religious scholars. The application provides an access to the database within advanced searching options and graphic visualization. All the results can be downloaded as a .bib file or a graphic picture.

Content of the Database
Texts and more

The core of this project is a large bibliographical dataset. It includes various types of the textual material, both primary sources and academic literature, such as books, articles, catalogues, doctoral thesis, dictionaries, etc. This massive of documents covers the period from the sixteen century to the most recent publications. The dataset includes entities in various languages, including English, Italian, French, Portuguese, German, Latin, etc.

This collection is based on the bibliographical materials, selected by the research group members within their personal projects. The further information about these projects is available on the web page of the group. Therefore, these titles correspond to a large variety of subject: from issues of the matrimonial law to the geographical concepts of the Roman Curia in Early Modern Period.

Approaches and methods

The keyword tree, which we used in the Smart Search and Graphs has four conceptual levels, from general concepts, such as Roman Curia and Praxis to the specific aspects of the Congregation of the Council activities and competences, such as Visit ad Limina and Discipline of the Clergy/

The keywords sections are divided by colours for a comfortable visual navigation. In addition, the search options include a possibility of search by character and numeric custom inputs, such as author, title, publsher and year.
